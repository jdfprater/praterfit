1 RM Max Protocol

Before you start a weight training program, it is a good idea to test your 1RM (1 rep max). This is the theoretical (or practical) amount of weight you can lift for just one rep with perfect form.

Follow these steps:

  1. Warm up thoroughly.
  2. Make sure you've had 24 hours of rest from your last lifting session.
  3. Perform of an exercise with a weight of which at least 5 repetitions can be achieved. Do likewise with all exercises.
  4. Incrementally add a small amount of weight over subsequent sets, of each exercise, until, at a certain weight, no repetition can be achieved. The weight lifted before this set would be the 1RM. Before each 1RM test set allow for 3-5 minutes rest.
  5. Document all 1RMs in a training diary.
  6. Re-test every 5-6 weeks to monitor training progress. The same equipment and exercises should be used during successive testings.

If you get something like 3 reps on your test, you can predict what your 1RM would be with a calculator like the one created above.
