Karvonen Formula for Prescribing Aerobic Exercise

Max Heart Rate Protocol

Use an 8" step (almost any step in your home or in a club will do) and perform a 3-minute step test.

Follow these instructions:

  1. After your warm-up, step up and down in a four-count sequence as follows: right foot up, left up, right down, left down. Each time you move a foot up or down, it counts as one step.
  2. Count "up, up, down, down" for one set, with 20 sets to the minute. It is very important that you don't speed up the pace--keep it regular.
  3. After 2 minutes, you'll need to monitor your heart rate for the last minute.
  4. To check, use either a heart rate monitor or check your pulse; count your heart beats for 6 seconds and multiply by 10 for beats per minute.
  5. Add to your last minute's heart rate average one of the following numbers:

    1. Poor Shape: +55 bpm

    2. Average Shape: +65 bpm

    3. Excellent Shape: +75 bpm

Your result should be pretty close to your Max HR. (My last-minute heart rate average might be something like 120 bpm, to which I'd add 75 bpm, bringing the total to 195 bpm.)
